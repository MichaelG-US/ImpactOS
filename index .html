01 — The Problem

Critical impact data is trapped in spreadsheets.

  • Excessive time lost to manual reporting and reconciliation.
  • Field data fragmented across KoboToolbox, DHIS2, Salesforce, SharePoint, and Excel.
  • Donors increasingly demand real-time impact visibility and accountability.
  • Advanced analytics and AI capacity are out of reach for most organizations.
02 — The Opportunity

An AI-native operations layer for the social sector.

  • AI can automate reporting, analytics, and operational intelligence end-to-end.
  • NGOs and humanitarian organizations urgently need efficiency gains under shrinking budgets.
  • AI-native systems dramatically reduce operational overhead per program dollar.
  • The development sector is shifting fast toward data-driven decision-making.

05 — AI Agent Architecture

Five specialized agents, one shared context.

R-01

Reporting Agent

Drafts donor reports, KPI summaries, and quarterly narratives against the organization's logframe.

summarize(indicators, period, donor_template)
Q-02

Data Quality Agent

Identifies missing values, schema drift, and inconsistencies before they propagate downstream.

scan(dataset).flag(anomalies)
G-03

Grant Intelligence Agent

Continuously scans funding opportunities and aligns them to your portfolio and capacity.

match(rfp, portfolio).score()
E-04

Executive Insights Agent

Answers strategic questions — programmatic, financial, and operational — in plain language.

ask("which regions slipped on objective 2?")
F-05

Field Operations Agent

Summarizes field reports, surfaces emerging risks, and triages incidents across country offices.

digest(field_reports, risk_lens)

07 — Why ImpactOS

Domain-deep, AI-native, sector-specific.

Most analytics tools are built for sales pipelines or hospital networks and retrofitted for the social sector. ImpactOS starts from M&E and humanitarian workflows — logframes, indicators, donor templates, partner reporting — and treats AI as the default interface, not a chatbot bolted on top.

  • Domain depth. Built by practitioners who have lived inside DHIS2, Kobo, and donor compliance.
  • AI-native workflows. Agents handle the busywork; humans do judgment.
  • Sector-specific. Indicators, partners, and grants are first-class objects, not generic rows.
  • Bridge. Closes the gap between technology teams and program operations.
